Cooperation Through Structured Disagreement

Like Madison's Constitution provides a framework for political disagreement, Legis Ledger provides infrastructure for epistemic disagreement—preserving sharp differences (pluralism) while encouraging convergence as evidence accumulates (Bayesian updating).

Wikipedia enables cooperation through exhaustive debate and eventual consensus.

This works for settled facts, but breaks down for contested facts, situational judgments, probabilistic claims, and health interventions.

We enable cooperation through structured disagreement and Bayesian convergence.

Sharp differences are preserved (stability through plurality), while confidence converges as evidence accumulates (dynamism through updating). No central authority decrees "the right answer"—evidence and formal validation determine confidence.

Every knowledge claim includes transparent provenance: who submitted it, what biases might exist, how to verify independently, and explicit acknowledgment of uncertainty.

Delay: 5 hours | Cause: Thunderstorm + crew shortage | Jurisdiction: EU

Conclusion

Likely entitled to €400 compensation

Weather alone qualifies as extraordinary circumstance, BUT airline must prove storm directly caused THIS delay. Crew shortage is NOT extraordinary. Courts split on mixed-cause cases.

Why Lower Confidence?

  • Fact-intensive: Outcome depends on airline's documentation of weather impact vs. crew issue timing
  • Mixed precedent: Some courts find for passengers, others for airlines in similar mixed-cause scenarios
  • Burden of proof: Airline must prove extraordinary circumstances, but passengers must challenge insufficient proof

Scientific Knowledge: Creatine Supplementation

Demonstrating canonical scientific knowledge (0.90 confidence) with comprehensive evidence hierarchy, transparent Bayesian reasoning, and full provenance disclosure.

Does creatine monohydrate supplementation increase lean body mass in resistance training?

90%
Confidence (10% chance wrong)
0% 25% 50% 75% 100%
Narrow End - Canonical

Conclusion

Yes: Creatine significantly increases lean body mass during resistance training

Effect Size: 0.7-1.5 kg additional lean body mass over 6-12 weeks compared to placebo

Mechanism: Increases muscle phosphocreatine stores → enables higher training volume/intensity → stimulates muscle protein synthesis

Safety: Excellent profile at 3-5g/day based on 25+ years of research

Evidence Hierarchy (Graded)

  • Grade A+ Candow et al. (2022) Meta-Analysis

    35 RCTs, 1,192 participants. Effect: +0.68 kg overall, +1.1-1.5 kg with resistance training. GRADE assessment: HIGH quality evidence.

  • Grade A+ Antonio et al. (2021) Systematic Review

    Comprehensive review of 500+ peer-reviewed publications. Confirms safety and efficacy. Addresses common misconceptions.

  • Grade A Kreider et al. (2017) ISSN Position Stand

    Official scientific consensus from International Society of Sports Nutrition. Authoritative endorsement of creatine monohydrate.

  • Grade B Harris et al. (1992) Mechanistic Study

    Foundational paper demonstrating creatine increases muscle creatine stores by 10-40%. Mechanism validated via muscle biopsy.

What Would Change Our Mind?

  • Large null RCT (N>1,000) with proper dosing/duration shows no effect → confidence drops to ~60%
  • Methodological flaw discovered: DEXA/BIA systematically overestimate lean mass in creatine users due to water retention → confidence drops to ~50%
  • Publication bias meta-analysis: Entire effect disappears when unpublished studies included → confidence drops to ~40%
  • Mechanism disproven: Creatine shown NOT to increase muscle phosphocreatine in follow-up studies → confidence drops to ~30%

Important Qualifications

  • Requires concurrent resistance training: Creatine alone has minimal effect on lean body mass. Effect depends on training stimulus.
  • Individual response varies: ~20-30% are "non-responders" (likely due to already-high baseline muscle creatine levels).
  • Modest but consistent effect: ~5-10% additional gain beyond training alone. Not a miracle supplement, but reliably effective.
  • Water retention component: Some early gains are intracellular water. Long-term gains reflect true muscle protein accretion.

⚠️ Not Medical Advice

This is educational information only. 90% confidence means 10% chance wrong—science is provisional. Individual results vary significantly. Consult healthcare provider before starting supplementation, especially if you have kidney disease or other health conditions.

Contraindications: Individuals with pre-existing kidney disease should consult physician. Generally recognized as safe (GRAS) for healthy adults at 3-5g/day.

Why This Matters

For Democracy

Citizens need reliable, transparent knowledge with quantified confidence that updates as evidence emerges. No false certainty. No hidden assumptions. Clear verification pathways.

For Science

The replication crisis shows science needs better infrastructure. This provides Bayesian confidence (not binary p-values), transparent reasoning, replication tracking, bias disclosure, and provenance transparency.

For Rauch's Constitution of Knowledge

This operationalizes liberal science: No final say (confidence < 1.0 always), community validation (multiple institutions weigh in), empirical checking (real-world outcomes tracked), error-seeking (falsifiability explicit), and transparent reasoning.
