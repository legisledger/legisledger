Sweet spot: $500k-1M at $2-2.5M pre-money = 20-33% dilution (reasonable for seed)

Valuation is negotiable and depends on:
1. Supply & Demand

Low demand (1 interested investor): Accept lower valuation
High demand (5+ interested investors): Hold firm or increase
Competitive process drives valuation up 20-40%

2. Strategic Value
If investor brings:

Customer introductions ‚Üí Worth 5-10% more dilution
Technical expertise (Bayesian professor) ‚Üí Worth 5% more
Just money ‚Üí Standard dilution

3. Your Leverage

How much do you need? (More need = weaker negotiating position)
Can you bootstrap longer? (Yes = stronger position)
Do you have alternatives? (Grants, revenue, other investors)

4. Geography

San Francisco: $3-4M typical for this stage
East Coast: $2-3M typical
Other regions: $1-2M typical

5. Investor Type

Angels/friends & family: Less dilution-sensitive, often accept $2-3M
Seed VCs: More data-driven, might push toward $1.5-2M
Strategic angels: Value alignment over valuation

Position at $2M pre-money initially
Rationale:

Conservative but defensible
Room to negotiate up (if multiple investors)
Won't scare away angels (not too high)
Respects your stage (pre-revenue)
Leaves room for Series A step-up (typically 2-3x)

But be flexible:
Accept $1.5M if:

Investor brings strategic value (CMU professor connections, customer intros)
Need to close quickly (Hatch Act timing)
Investor takes majority of round (lead investor)

Hold firm at $2.5M if:

Multiple investors interested (competitive)
Strong advisor validation (Steve says valuation fair)
Traction improving (website traffic, inbound interest)

Go to $3M if:

Exceptional investor (top-tier VC, strategic value)
After 6 pilots signed (customer validation)
After Steve introduces customers (reduces sales risk)

Don't Lead with Valuation

Bad approach:
"We're raising $500k at a $2M pre-money valuation."

Better approach:
"We're raising $500k to hit these milestones: [list]. Based on comparable pre-seed rounds and our progress to date, we're thinking $2-2.5M pre-money is fair, but we're open to discussing what makes sense."

Investor: "Usually $1-2M"
You: "That's in line with what we're thinking. Given our working MVPs and strategic advisor, we're leaning toward the higher end of that range. Does $2M pre-money feel reasonable?"
