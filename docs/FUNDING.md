## Mission

To build federated knowledge infrastructure that enables **cooperation through structured disagreement** - where institutions share expertise with transparent confidence, provenance, and Bayesian updating.

---

## Why Fund This?

### The Problem

**Current state:** Citizens can't reliably access expert knowledge:
- Wikipedia requires consensus (fails for contested facts)
- Google search prioritizes SEO over expertise
- AI chatbots hallucinate or cite sources without confidence levels
- Government guidance is siloed and hard to find
- Scientific knowledge lacks provenance transparency

**Cost to society:** Millions of citizens make decisions with incomplete, biased, or false information.

### Our Solution

**Federated knowledge infrastructure** where:
- Institutions publish expert opinions with quantified confidence
- Multiple viewpoints preserved (structured pluralism)
- Real-world outcomes validate claims (Bayesian updates)
- Users see provenance ("who says this, what are their biases?")
- No central authority controls truth (capture-resistant architecture)

### Impact

**For democracy:** Informed citizens making evidence-based decisions  
**For science:** Replication tracking and transparent uncertainty  
**For policy:** Honest assessments that update with evidence  
**For institutions:** Reduced support burden, increased trust

## Governance Safeguards: Why Funding â‰  Control

### The Capture Problem

**Traditional model:** Funders â†’ influence governance â†’ capture infrastructure  
**Why it fails:** Compromises mission, institutions leave, network dies

### Our Structural Solution

We make capture **mathematically impossible** through architecture:

**1. Revenue Diversity (Enforced by Charter)**
- No single funder may exceed 20% of annual budget
- Enforced by non-profit bylaws, not discretion
- Board members must disclose all funding relationships

**2. Open Source + Fork Rights**
- All code: Apache 2.0 license
- All data: CC-BY or CC0
- Anyone can fork if original captured
- Federation protocol ensures interoperability

**3. Decentralized Identity**
- Institutions control their own DIDs
- Infrastructure can't censor content
- Can't force updates to claims
- No coercive power

**4. Transparent Finances**
- All transactions >$10k published within 30 days
- Quarterly financial reports (public)
- Annual independent audit (required)
- GitHub repository for all governance decisions

**5. Board Structure**
- 7 seats: 3 institutional, 2 technical, 2 domain experts
- No "funder seats" (funding doesn't buy governance control)
- Simple majority for operations, 2/3 for charter amendments
- Dissolution requires unanimous vote

## Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Institutional Adoption
**Risk:** Institutions don't participate  
**Mitigation:** Value propositions by type, starting with easy domains (passenger rights, well-established science)  
**Status:** 2 MVPs complete show value

### Risk 2: Financial Sustainability
**Risk:** Revenue doesn't materialize  
**Mitigation:** Multiple revenue streams, starting with proven commercial use cases (travel industry), open-source fallback  
**Status:** API pricing validated with early partners

### Risk 3: Governance Capture
**Risk:** Funders or admins take control  
**Mitigation:** Structural impossibility through architecture (see Governance Safeguards)  
**Status:** Bylaws drafted, fork rights guaranteed

### Risk 4: Quality Control
**Risk:** Bad actors publish misinformation  
**Mitigation:** Tiered visibility based on track record, Bayesian updates expose miscalibration  
**Status:** Quality tier system designed and tested

### Risk 5: Technical Complexity
**Risk:** Bayesian reasoning too complex for users  
**Mitigation:** Excellent UX, "explained like I'm 5" content, progressive disclosure  
**Status:** Creatine example shows clear explanation works
